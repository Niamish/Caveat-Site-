Master Services Agreement Annotated by Caveat · 7 flags
§1

Term & Renewal

This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for an initial term of twelve (12) months. Thereafter it shall automatically renew for successive twelve-month terms unless either party gives written notice of non-renewal no fewer than ninety (90) days prior to the end of the then-current term⚑.

§2

Fees & Increases

Customer shall pay the fees set out in the applicable Order Form. Provider may increase fees upon renewal by providing notice, with increases not to exceed the greater of 7% or the change in CPI⚑. Invoices are due net thirty (30) days; overdue amounts accrue interest at 1.5% per month.

§3

Limitation of Liability

Except for the excluded claims below, each party's total aggregate liability shall not exceed the fees paid in the three (3) months preceding the claim⚑. In no event shall Provider be liable for any indirect, incidental, or consequential damages, including loss of data, even if advised of the possibility⚑.

§4

Indemnification

Customer shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Provider from any and all claims arising out of Customer's use of the Services, without limitation⚑. Provider's indemnity, by contrast, is capped at the liability ceiling in §3 and excludes claims relating to data handling should expressly cover IP and data-handling claims.

§5

Intellectual Property

Provider retains all right, title, and interest in the Services. Customer grants Provider a perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free license to use Customer data, in aggregated and de-identified form, to improve the Services⚑.

§6

Termination

Either party may terminate for material breach uncured after thirty (30) days' written notice. Upon termination, Customer may export its data for a period of thirty (30) days, after which Provider may delete it⚑. Sections 3, 4, and 5 survive termination.

§7

Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to conflict-of-laws principles. The parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in Wilmington, Delaware.

Caveat's notes

Bites§1

Auto-renewal with a 90-day window. You're locked in for another year unless you remember to cancel three months early. Calendar it the day you sign, or negotiate the notice down to 30 days.

Watch§2

Uncapped-ish price increases. "Greater of 7% or CPI" means the floor is 7%, not the ceiling. Ask for a hard cap and a fixed first-renewal price.

Bites§3

Liability capped at 3 months of fees. If something goes badly wrong, your recovery is tiny relative to the harm. Push for 12 months of fees, at minimum.

Bites§3

Consequential damages excluded — including data loss. If their failure wipes your data, this clause says you can't claim the downstream cost. Carve data loss out of the exclusion.

Bites§4

One-way, uncapped indemnity. You defend them "without limitation"; their indemnity is capped. Make it mutual and subject to the same cap.

Watch§5

Perpetual license to your data. Aggregated and de-identified is common, but "perpetual, irrevocable" survives termination forever. Confirm the de-identification standard and a deletion right.

Standard§6

30-day export window. Market-standard. Fine as written — just diarise the export deadline so you don't lose the data.

The register

The clauses that quietly move risk onto you.

ClauseDefault severityWhat Caveat watches for
Auto-renewalBitesLong notice windows, silent rollovers, renewal-only price hikes.
Limitation of liabilityBitesCaps set at a few months of fees, consequential-damage and data-loss carve-outs.
IndemnificationBitesOne-way duties, "without limitation," defence obligations larger than the cap.
Unilateral amendmentBites"We may modify these terms at any time" with deemed acceptance on continued use.
IP & data licenseWatchPerpetual, irrevocable grants; vague de-identification; training-on-your-data rights.
Exclusivity / non-solicitWatchBroad scope, no geography or time limit, hidden inside boilerplate.
Assignment & change of controlWatchThey can assign freely; you cannot — even in an acquisition.
